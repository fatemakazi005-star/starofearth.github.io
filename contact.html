Common Questions

FAQ

Is all the science on this site accurate? ▾
Yes — every scientific claim is reviewed by our science team and sourced from peer-reviewed literature or verified by reputable institutions such as NASA, ESA, and the EHT Collaboration. If you spot an error, please use the contact form above.
Can I use your content for my school or university? ▾
Absolutely. All text content is free to use for educational purposes with attribution. SVG diagrams and assets are available under a Creative Commons license. Contact us if you need higher-resolution exports or formal licensing.
Is the source code available? ▾
Yes! The entire website is open source and available on GitHub. We welcome pull requests for bug fixes, improvements, and new content. See the link in the contact section above.
How often is the site updated? ▾
We aim to update the site within a few weeks of major discoveries — new EHT images, gravitational wave events, or significant papers published in Nature or Science. Sign up for the newsletter to be notified.
I'm a researcher. Can I contribute content? ▾
We'd love that! We're always looking for researchers and science communicators who want to contribute articles, visualizations, or expert quotes. Reach out via the collaboration email above.
